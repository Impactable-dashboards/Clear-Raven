00 / The operating principle

Spend warm before you spend cold.

Every dollar is allocated by intent. The closer an audience is to already knowing Clear Raven, the cheaper and higher-converting it is, a pattern Impactable has measured across 200+ B2B SaaS ad accounts. The catch: Clear Raven starts close to cold, with little warm inventory and no detectable ad history. So the first job of cold and ABM spend is to manufacture a warm pool fast, then let retargeting do the efficient closing work. Click each rung to see the benchmark and the role it plays.

01 / Channel one

LinkedIn native architecture.

LinkedIn is the qualification and nurture engine, the only platform that lets a warm audience be filtered down to the exact ICP by title, seniority, and function. Built in three tiers. Always disable Audience Expansion. Always exclude competitors, existing customers, and non-ICP roles.

Tier 1 Warm retargeting highest priority

Thin today, which is the whole reason for the cold-start sequence. Stand the Insight Tag up on day one so the pool starts filling immediately. Start broad when building each pool, then layer ICP filters once it has size. Restrict the retargeting window to 90 days to keep saturation high.

02 / Channel two

Third-party signal overlays.

Native LinkedIn filters inevitably capture people outside the ICP. Signal overlays move targeting from "looks like the ICP" to "is actively in-market right now." These are the inputs to the Clay-built ABM list. Pick the top two or three, score accounts against them, then upload as a Matched Audience. More on the method: The Power of Third-Party Custom Audiences.

03 / Channel three

Paid search capture.

Google captures demand that already exists. For Clear Raven the highest-value query in the entire market is the switcher in motion: someone typing "[competitor] alternative." Competitor conquest runs in its own campaign, exact match, with its own budget, so it never bleeds into broad terms. The intent pyramid below is funded top-down.

/// google: where to start
Fund the top of the pyramid first.
  1. Start with 5–6 keywords: the two strongest competitor-conquest terms, "cmms software for [top vertical]," "preventive maintenance software," "cmms software with no per-user fees," and brand defense.
  2. Run conquest in its own exact-match campaign with a separate budget so it never competes with broad terms for spend.
  3. Point every high-intent ad at a conviction landing page built on the no-gating + transparent-pricing message, not the generic homepage.

04 / Budget framework

The split moves as the warm pool fills.

Clear Raven is a cold start: minimal warm inventory, no detectable ad history. So warm gets a heavier-than-usual share early, precisely because the ABM and cold layers are manufacturing that warm pool in real time. As retargeting pools fill over the first 60–90 days, the split rebalances toward the standard warm-led allocation. Toggle to watch the shift.

/// budget allocation · month over month
40% · Warm
35% · ABM
25% · Cold

Month 1 (cold start): 40 / 35 / 25. Warm is over-weighted relative to its small size because every ABM and cold impression is feeding the retargeting pool. The goal of month one is not conversion, it is manufacturing a warm audience worth retargeting.
